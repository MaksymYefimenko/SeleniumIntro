    A selenide is a chemical compound containing a selenium anion with oxidation number of 2 (Se ), much as sulfur does in a sulfide. The chemistry of the selenides and sulfides is similar. Similar to sulfide, in aqueous solution, the selenide ion, Se , is prevalent only in very basic conditions. In neutral conditions, hydrogen selenide ion, HSe , is most common. In acid conditions, hydrogen selenide, H2Se, is formed.
